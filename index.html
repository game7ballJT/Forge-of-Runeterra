What Is Forge of Runeterra?

Forge of Runeterra is a free, 100% web-native League of Legends companion site — unlike desktop overlay tools such as Blitz.gg, which runs as a background application monitoring the League client, or U.GG's separate Live Game Tool install, Forge of Runeterra provides champion builds, rune pages, tier lists, and patch breakdowns directly in your browser, with no download required.

It's built for players who want fast, reliable answers — "what should I build on this champion," "what changed this patch," "what counters this matchup" — without installing software just to get a build recommendation. Champion, item, and rune data is pulled live from Riot Games' own Data Dragon API, so it updates automatically as soon as a new patch ships, rather than waiting on a manual content refresh.

How Forge of Runeterra differs from desktop overlay tools

  • No installation required. Tools like Blitz.gg and U.GG's Live Game Tool run as standalone desktop applications that monitor the League client in the background. Forge of Runeterra runs entirely in a browser tab.
  • No champ-select overlay, by design. This is a reference site you check before or after a game, not an in-game overlay — there's no background process polling the League Client API while you play.
  • Lightweight by default. A single page load gets you the full champion roster, tier list, and build database — no separate app to keep updated, no extra RAM or CPU overhead while you're in a match.

Forge of Runeterra is not affiliated with or endorsed by Riot Games. Champion and item data is sourced through Riot's public Data Dragon API, the same data feed used by most third-party League of Legends sites.

Forge of Runeterra vs. Blitz.gg vs. U.GG

The biggest practical difference is installation: Blitz.gg and U.GG's in-game overlay both run as standalone desktop applications, while Forge of Runeterra works entirely inside a browser tab. Patch 26.13 Notes

Released June 24, 2026

Patch 26.13 is the final tuning pass before MSI 2026, and it shows: most of the balance work this patch targets champions likely to see competitive play. The headline addition is Locke, the new Ashen Exorcist. Senna, Bard, Brand, Cassiopeia, K'Sante, and Rek'Sai all come down a notch, while LeBlanc, Draven, Kai'Sa, Olaf, Poppy, and Aphelios get a push back into relevance. On the systems side, Last Hit Indicators are rolling out to Normal Draft and Ranked, and Imperial Mandate's build path has been reworked.

▲ Champion Buffs (7) ▼ Champion Nerfs (6) ◆ Rune Changes ○ System Changes ★ New Skins
Rune & Keystone Changes
Imperial Mandate REWORKED
Slot: Mythic-tier support item
Build path smoothed out for supports who found the previous component route awkward: Amp Tome + Bandleglass Mirror + Amp Tome + gold, replacing the old Blasting Wand + Bandleglass Mirror line. Power is shifted along with the new path rather than removed outright.

Developer insight: the old Blasting Wand route gave a small AP spike early but left supports without a defensive component until the full build completed. The Amp Tome path trades that spike for steadier mana sustain across the build — worth knowing if you're used to the old timing.

Last Hit Indicators NEW SYSTEM
Modes: Normal Draft & Ranked Summoner's Rift
A white indicator now shows on a minion's health bar when it's killable with a basic attack — already live in Swiftplay and Co-op vs. AI, now expanding to the modes where most players actually rank up.
Summon Aery NERFED
Shielding: 30–100 (L1–18) (+10% bonus AD)(+5% AP) → 20–100 (L1–18) (+10% bonus AD)(+5% AP)
Early game defensive output reduced so trades can stick against enchanter duos in lane. Late game shielding unchanged — Lulu, Karma, Soraka are not significantly impacted at full build.
Guardian BUFFED
Cooldown: 90–40s (L1–18) → 75–40s (L1–18)
Shield ratios adjusted to help tankier users — Braum, Taric — survive the laning phase against aggressive lane bullies. The reduced early cooldown means more Guardian activations in the first 10 minutes of the game.
Aftershock BUFFED
Early game resistances and damage output buffed slightly. Helps aggressive engage tanks — Malphite, Leona, Nautilus — trade more effectively during the laning phase without waiting for their first component item.
System & Client Changes
Normal Draft — 24/7 in ME, OC, SG

Normal Draft queues expanded from windowed availability to 24/7 in Middle East, Oceania, and Singapore regions. This follows meaningful queue time improvements across these regions since late 2025.

Apex Duo Restrictions — Ongoing

Duo queuing remains restricted for Apex-tier players in regions where it was disabled at Season 2 start. Riot is actively monitoring high-tier match quality before re-enabling. Update expected in Patch 26.13 or 26.14.

Honor & Penalty Visibility in Social Panel

Your current Honor level, active restrictions, and redemption steps are now visible directly in your social panel. Makes tracking Honor progress and understanding behavior impact much easier without navigating menus.

Teleport Shield — Health Adjusted

Teleport Shield health value adjusted to better align with early-game trading patterns. This is a tuning change, not a fundamental rework of the mechanic. The change primarily affects how much protection laner recall timing provides.

ARAM Subscription Test — Brazil & Oceania

Players in BR and OCE can pay 1350 RP to unlock most Epic-tier and below skins for 30 days, usable in ARAM and ARAM: Mayhem. Regional test only — may expand to additional regions based on results.

"My Leagues" Feature Removed

The low-use "My Leagues" section has been removed from the Ranked area of the client to simplify navigation. All information previously shown there is still accessible through the main Ranked hub.

Experimental Hexplate — Varus Interaction

Changes to Experimental Hexplate specifically target Varus's dominance in top lane with lethality builds. His primary on-hit ADC role in bot lane is less affected. Combined with the direct W on-hit nerf, this is designed to reduce his flex presence without gutting his bot lane identity.
